This inventory is based on `gdpr-audit/00_data-processing-brief.md` and the project source. It distinguishes company-level intelligence from individual-level data. Company-level data is generally not personal data, but it becomes personal data where it contains named contacts, work email addresses, inferred attributes, or engagement history linked to identifiable people.

The external processing chain uses US-based commercial services including Tavily, OpenAI, Anthropic, Hunter.io, Supabase, Resend, and Slack. For GDPR transfer analysis, this should be treated as a full-stack US transfer chain with no adequacy decision for standard commercial data flows.

## Purpose Creep Flags

Hunter.io contact data appears to originate from information published or aggregated for professional networking, discoverability, or business contact purposes. The project repurposes that data for automated prospecting, CRM creation, AI-personalized outreach, engagement tracking, follow-up sequencing, and AI-based reply sentiment/intent profiling.

That is a material expansion from contact discoverability into behavioral profiling and automated sales treatment. Under GDPR Article 6(4), this should be flagged as likely purpose incompatibility unless the controller can document a compatible lawful basis, balancing test, transparency notice, objection handling, and suppression process.

The risk is higher because the live workflow does not enforce the existing `opt_out_domains` table before contact collection or CRM/report creation. That means suppression intent exists in the schema but does not currently constrain the processing chain at the point where purpose expansion occurs.
